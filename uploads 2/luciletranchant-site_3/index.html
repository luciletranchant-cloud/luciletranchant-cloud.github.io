Research, Narrative Intelligence & Responsible AI

I study how information moves, what audiences trust, and when narratives become risk. Then I turn those findings into decisions, policy and working systems.

3 production AI workflows · 50+ editions shipped · 30+ languages monitored · deterministic gating + human review

I lead communications monitoring and analysis at the International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies (IFRC), working across a global network of 191 National Societies, and previously worked on the digital intelligence team at UNICEF in New York. My work combines real-time social and media analysis, executive insights, measurement and harmful-information research, from understanding emerging narratives during emergencies to helping leadership make sense of complex reputation questions.

I also build AI-assisted research workflows when existing tools cannot answer the question well enough. My work sits between research, communications, policy and applied AI: finding the signal, testing what it means, and making it useful to the people who need to act.
